The Wealthy Hand-to-Mouth

Authors: Kaplan, G., Violante, G. L., & Weidner, J. (2014)

Presenter: Nicole Golden

Long Definition

Wealthy hand-to-mouth HHs hold little or no liquid wealth (cash, checking, savings accounts), despite owning sizable amounts of illiquid assets (housing, retirement accounts, with a transaction cost).

- Kaplan et al (2014)

Why Wealthy HTM short in cash?

  • Put money in illiquid assets (housing, retirement accounts) that offer high returns
    → little cash on hand
  • Recently purchased a house
    → liquid wealth used as a down payment

For wealthy HTM, smoothing consumption means:

  • Frequently paying a transaction cost to access their illiquid wealth
  • Holding large balances of cash and hence forgoing the high return on the illiquid savings
  • Obtaining credit at expensive interest rates

Finding 1

  • Both poor and wealthy HTM have large MPCs
  • Wealthy HTM responds strongly to temporary government transfers
    → Boost economy through spending

Policy: Targeting

  • Fiscal stimulus programs should target poor HTM

    • → Should also target wealthy HTM
    → Face similar constraint on spending

Finding 2

  • Small stimulus payments
    → wealthy & poor HTM HHs increase their spending by similar amounts
  • Larger stimulus payments
    → wealthy HTM increase consumption much less than poor HTM

Policy: Size of Intervention

  • The bigger the stimulus, the bigger the effect on spending
    → Wealthy HTM more likely to save some of government windfall
    → reducing its effectiveness to boost economy

Finding 3

  • Wealthy HTM weather income fluctuations, not dip into assets to smooth shocks
    → smoothing shocks means holding large cash & foregone high return on illiquid assets.

Policy: State-dependence
