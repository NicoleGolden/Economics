Discussion:

The Market for Financial Advice

Authors: Mullainathan, S., Noeth, M., & Schoar, A. (2012)

Presenter: Nicole Golden

Background

  • Past literature: HH bad at choosing portfolios
  • 73% individuals consult financial advisers before purchasing
  • "Good advice": low cost, diversified, index-fund approach

An Audit Study

  • Want to test quality of advice to retail investors
  • Lower end (pure investment) retail advisers: paid based on fees they generate, not performance of portfolio
  • Question: Do advisers de-bias clients / exaggerate baises that help max their fees?

4 Treatments

  • "Chasing fund returns": 30% in one sector, misaligned interests → max fees, less diversified
  • "Employer stocks": 30% to company stock, aligned interests → restructure portfolio
  • Efficient US portfolio: low cost, diversified, index funds
  • "Cash scenario": hold CDs (control)

Behavioral Bias

  • Fail to de-bias clients / exaggerate existing biases
  • Push for funds that maximize fees
  • Some cases: higher expected fees, less diversification, reduce expected returns
