Research Question

  • Do extended payment plans improve financial health?
  • Wang and Burke (2022):
    • Texas, Austin, Dallas require disclosing cost of payday loans vs other credit products
    • Led to significant decline in payday loan volume
  • Contribution: a broader payday loan policy applied to more states

Overview

  • Extended payment plans improve financial health by reducing:
    • Past due amount by $25
    • Original charge-off by $49
    • Delinquency rate by 2.9%
    • Charge-off or debt-in-collections rate by 2.7%

Background: Payday Loans

  • Small-dollar, legal in 29 states (2020)
  • Loan limit $500, unsecured, repay in next payday
  • High APRs: 300% ~ 500% (credit cards: 12% ~ 30%)
  • Features: "cash, quick, fast, easy, emergency bills"
  • Rollover: charges will double!
  • "Debt Traps"

Background:
Extended Payment Plans

  • Key features:
    • Repay in 3 ~ 4 installments (salient) with zero charges
    • Minimum repayment term 60 ~ 90 days
    • Limit use to once a year
    • Eligible only if reach a threshold of rollovers
    • Require lenders to disclose availability of the plans

Data

  • Clarity Payday Loan Data
    • Coverage: 60 million or 70% of non-prime consumers
    • Period: 2015 ~ 2020
    • Borrowers' characteristics: age, net monthly income, pay frequency, housing status, months at address
    • Financial health: amount past due, original charge-off, delinquency rate, charge-off or debt-in-collections rate
    • Final data: aggregated at quarterly level for each state (14 control and 5 treated states, in 2020 dollars)
  • Legal status Data
    • Refer to each state's deferred deposit transaction law

Model: Synthetic DiD

    \[ \tau^{SDID} = argmin_{\mu, \alpha, \beta, \tau} \Bigl\{\sum_{i = 1}^{N} \sum_{t = 1}^{T} (Y_{it} - \mu - \alpha_{i} - \beta_{t} - \tau D_{it})^{2} \hat{w}_{i} \hat{\lambda}_{t} \Bigl\} \]
  • \( Y_{it} \) = financial health in state \(i \), quarter \(t \)
  • \( \mu \) = intercept
  • \( \alpha_{i} \), \( \beta_{t} \) = state, quarter fixed effects
  • \( \tau \) = ATT
  • \( D_{it} \) = 1 if passed extended payment plans, 0 otherwise
  • \( \hat{w}_{i} \), \( \hat{\lambda}_{t} \) = unit, time weights

Synthetic DiD: Unit Weights

    \[ w^{sdid} = argmin\| \bar{y}_{pre, treat} - Y_{pre, control} * w_{control}\|_{2}^{2} + \zeta T_{pre}\|w_{control}\|^2_{2} \]
  • (Pre-treat) Generate unit weights that minimize difference between outcomes of treated & weighted average of control units
  • Gives parallel trend
  • Exploits advantages of DiD & SC

Synthetic DiD: Time Weights

    \[ \lambda^{sdid} = argmin\| \bar{y}_{post, control} - \lambda_{pre}Y_{pre, control} + \lambda_{0}\|_{2}^{2} \]
  • (Control units) Generate time weights that minimize difference of post-treat & weighted average of pre-treat outcomes
  • Increase precision of treatment effect estimates

Synthetic DiD: Staggered Adoption

    \[ \frac{8}{65}ATT_{DE} + \frac{5}{65}ATT_{FL} + \frac{22}{65}ATT_{LA} + \frac{13}{65}ATT_{NV} + \frac{17}{65}ATT_{UT} \]
  • Run synthetic DiD for each treated state
  • Get 5 ATTs
  • Generate final ATT by reweighting each ATT with ratio of treated period and total periods

Conclusions

  • Payday loan borrowers prone to "debt traps"
  • Using best existing coverage of Clarity payday loan data
  • Uncover the effect of extended payment plans on financial health
  • These plans indeed have positive effects on financial health

Limitations & Future Work

  • Limitations:
    • Only 5 treated states (10 excluded)
    • More comprehensive study if data available
  • Future work:
    • Both low & high income borrow payday loans
    • Both may experience (-) income volatility
    • Study heterogeneous treatment effect
